Waso Learn - EdTech

Waso Learn, a social business under Strategy First Education Group, offers comprehensive online learning for KG to Grade 12 students. It provides curriculum-aligned lessons taught by experienced teachers to support ongoing studies.

RESPONSIBILITIES

UX UI Interaction Art Direction

PLATFORM

Mobile Android TV

THE TEAM

1 Department Head 1 Project Manager 1 Product Designer 7 Developers 1 Illustrator

CHALLENGES, SOLUTIONS, AND IMPACT

The initial design of Waso Learn was quite straightforward. It essentially took a traditional learning product idea and transferred it to a digital platform, resulting in a rather dull UI for an EdTech product. However, I observed that some parents and students preferred this simple and straightforward design. Those who were not very tech-savvy found the simple design helpful in avoiding complex and overwhelming interfaces. Despite this, our primary target is KG to G12 students, so we focused on researching their needs and motivations.

Image: The initial design of Waso Learn

After holding meetings with stakeholders and gathering user feedback, we considered two potential solutions: making a major update to the current product or creating an entirely new product. We decided to implement both ideas and efficiently proceeded with updating the product into an all-new version.

In the new version, we planned to establish an emotional connection with users and build a brand identity for the product by incorporating storytelling elements with a strong backbone story. Waso Learn’s chosen mascot is a rabbit, so we developed a character called the Wise Old Rabbit, who lives on the Moon and is passionate about teaching his family of rabbits through the Waso Learn app. This narrative attracted young learners’ attention and generated motivation for those accustomed to traditional learning methods.

Image: Redesigned version of Waso Learn

Representing lessons and grades as small villages and individual houses helped reduce the stress children felt about their studies. Additionally, when students completed lessons and exercises, they earned carrots based on their scores, which they could use to upgrade their houses. This gamification feature significantly increased engagement and retention rates.

Image: Redesigned version of Waso Learn

While we effectively addressed the challenges related to children, there were still issues with parents, particularly concerning the payment process. Credit/debit card usage is relatively low in Myanmar, and only Gen Z is generally proficient in using online payment gateways. A significant percentage of parents struggled with these platforms, so we had to rely on local payment platforms and handle some payment processes manually. This led to numerous technical errors and complaints. To ease parents' anxiety and stress, we provided step-by-step tutorials, made the payment success and failure states more visible with an updated design, and reduced the issues by about 80%.

Image: Little nuns using the Waso Learn TV app at a monastic school as part of CSR initiative

Although the Waso Learn mobile app successfully meets user needs, it still faces limitations in classroom settings, particularly due to screen size and usability constraints. The small screen restricts content visibility, while navigation challenges make it harder for students to engage effectively with lessons. These factors hinder the overall learning experience, especially when multiple students are involved.

To address this, we proposed developing a Waso Learn TV application as a strategic solution. Leveraging larger screens, this platform offers a more immersive and interactive learning experience. It enhances visual content, optimizes navigation, and improves interactivity, making lessons more accessible and engaging. By tapping into television interfaces, the TV app aims to create a seamless and enriched educational experience tailored to students' needs.

Image: Waso Learn for Android TVs

At present, the relevant product designers and the development team of the Waso Learn continue to work on refining the product.
